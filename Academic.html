Academic Resources

Academic Advising Overview

Academic advising is a process where students envision, execute, and reflect on their academic plans with guidance from a qualified UMSI Academic Advisor. Students share their goals, challenges, and questions with their academic advisor, and their academic advisor contributes knowledge of university and program requirements, policies, and processes. Together, they develop an academic plan that aligns with the student's personal and professional goals.

What can your academic advisor help with?

  1. Creating an individualized academic plan
  2. Interpreting an academic policy or procedure
  3. Discussing concerns about a particular course
  4. Connecting with student support resources and services
  5. Navigating a disruption to your BSI experience

BSI Degree Requirements

Understanding the degree

This page will help you understand the overall structure of the BSI Degree and what you need to consider in your broad degree planning process.

Total 120 Credits Needed to Graduate with BSI Degree

Your Time Before UMSI: 55-60+ Credits

This is all of the credits you brought to UMICH (AP/IB/Transfer credits from other institutions) and all of the credits you have taken since starting your time here at UMICH.

All credit is accepted and applied to the appropriate categories when it transfers to your UMSI Career.

Your Time at UMSI: 60 Upper Level Credits

45 Upper level SI Credits

45/60 Upper Level Credits are required to be SI specific credits. All courses must be at the 300 level or higher (Except SI 206).

15 Upper level Credits

15/60 Upper Level Credits can be SI or Non-SI coursework as long as they are 300+ level, they count!
